# Pisgah National Forest — Appalachian Ranger District

Headquarters in Mars Hill, NC. Accessed via Barnardsville and Dillingham Rd. The Big Ivy / Coleman Boundary area is in the northeast corner of Buncombe County — one of the wildest and most biologically diverse sections of Pisgah NF, with 30+ miles of trails for hiking, biking, and horses. Sits at the base of Mt. Mitchell and Craggy Gardens (peaks over 6,000 ft).

## Why Two Names?

- **Big Ivy**: Official name, from the Big Ivy River (fed by Carter and Dillingham creeks). "Ivy" is the Appalachian name for mountain laurel, which lines the creek corridors.
- **Coleman Boundary**: The longtime local name — almost certainly a historical landowner's name or old survey boundary marker predating Forest Service acquisition. Still used interchangeably by locals.

> **Note:** Area temporarily closed due to Hurricane Helene damage. Check [fs.usda.gov](https://www.fs.usda.gov/r08/northcarolina/recreation/big-ivy-area-coleman-boundary) before visiting.

## Swimming

- **Big Ivy Creek**: Cold, clear mountain creek running through the Coleman Boundary area. Multiple access points along Dillingham Rd.

## Dispersed Camping

Dispersed camping available throughout the Coleman Boundary. Remote and lightly visited compared to the Pisgah Ranger District.
