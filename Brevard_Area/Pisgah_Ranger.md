# Pisgah National Forest — Pisgah Ranger District

Headquarters in Pisgah Forest, NC (~20 miles from Lake Toxaway home base). The closest and most-visited Pisgah district. Contains Looking Glass Rock, Black Balsam Knob, the Art Loeb Trail, and the Davidson River corridor. US-276 is the main access road.

## Swimming

- **Avery Creek**: Clear, cold mountain creek with multiple pools along the Avery Creek Trail. Park at the Pisgah Stables / Pisgah Center for Wildlife Education trailhead off FR 477 (Avery Creek Rd). Good swimming holes within 0.5–1 mile of the trailhead.
- **Davidson River**: Most popular swimming in the district. Multiple access points along Davidson River Campground (US-276). Wide, shallow river with sandy patches.
- **Looking Glass Creek**: Along US-276 south of the campground. Several roadside pull-offs with good access.

## Dispersed Camping

Dispersed camping is permitted on National Forest land outside of developed recreation areas; stay 150 ft from water, trails, and roads.

- **Avery Creek corridor**: Several established dispersed sites along the creek. **AC8 is our favorite** — well-shaded with good creek access.
